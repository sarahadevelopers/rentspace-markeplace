Cost Breakdown: Furnishing a 1-Bedroom Airbnb in Syokimau for Under KSh 300k (2026)

May 1, 2026 8 min read 2,450 views

Syokimau’s short‑stay market remains hot – with travellers from JKIA, SGR, and businesses driving demand. But many new hosts overspend on furniture, killing their ROI. The reality is: you can fully furnish a **stylish, guest‑ready 1‑bedroom Airbnb in Syokimau for under KSh 300,000** and recover your investment in **6‑10 months** with smart pricing. This guide breaks down every item, costs, and the exact ROI strategies we use at RentSpace.

Why Syokimau is Perfect for a 1‑Bedroom Airbnb

  • Proximity to JKIA (10‑15 min) and SGR station (5‑10 min) – constant transit traffic.
  • Business travellers, airline crew, and tourists prefer affordable, clean short‑stay units.
  • Average nightly rate for a decent 1‑bedroom: KES 3,500 – 5,000. At 70% occupancy, that’s **KSh 73,500 – 105,000 monthly revenue**.

ROI Calculation – How to Recover in 6‑10 Months

Assume you buy an existing 1‑bedroom unit in Greatwall or Katani for KSh 3.5M (or rent it for 15‑20k/month and sublet – check your lease). For this analysis, focus only on furnishing cost, not purchase price.

  • Furnishing cost: KSh 280,000 (mid‑point).
  • Target nightly rate (after fees, e.g., Airbnb 15%): KSh 4,000.
  • Expected occupancy in Syokimau (first year): 65‑75%.
  • Monthly nights booked: 20 nights (67% occupancy) → revenue = 20 * 4,000 = KSh 80,000.
  • Monthly operating costs (cleaning, utilities, consumables, WiFi, platform fee already factored into net rate? Let’s separate):
    • Cleaning (per turnover): ~KSh 1,000 per booking. With 20 bookings: 20,000.
    • Electricity/water: ~3,000.
    • Consumables (toilet paper, soap, coffee): 2,000.
    • Total monthly costs ≈ 25,000.
  • Net monthly income: 80,000 – 25,000 = 55,000.
  • Payback period for furnishing: 280,000 / 55,000 ≈ 5.1 months.
  • At 60% occupancy (18 nights): revenue 72,000 – costs 25,000 = 47,000 → payback ≈ 6 months.

Even with conservative estimates, you recover your furnishing investment in **6‑8 months**. After that, it’s pure profit (excluding mortgage/rent).

Strategies to Maximise ROI & Recover Faster

  • Dynamic pricing – Use PriceLabs or Airbnb’s smart pricing. Lower rates mid‑week (3,200‑3,500), raise on weekends (5,000‑6,000).
  • Offer weekly/monthly discounts – 15% off for 7+ nights, 30% off for 28+ nights attracts longer stays, reducing turnover costs.
  • Profile for airport & transit guests – Emphasise proximity to JKIA and SGR, provide shuttle contact numbers. These guests rarely haggle.
  • Automate check‑in with a smart lock – Saves time and impresses guests. (Add KSh 8,000 – worth it, add to budget).
  • List on multiple platforms – Booking.com, Agoda, and direct bookings via social media.
  • Collect 5‑star reviews early – Provide a small welcome snack or handwritten note; good reviews increase search ranking and allow higher rates.

Where to Shop for Affordable Furniture in Syokimau & Nairobi

  • Toi Market (Ngong Road) – Second‑hand sofas, tables, decor – high quality at low cost (bargain).
  • Jiji.co.ke / Facebook Marketplace – Sellers often offload almost‑new furniture at 40‑60% off retail.
  • Vitabella / Rooms & Spaces – For new mattresses and bed frames (affordable range).
  • Naivas / Carrefour – Basic kitchenware and linens.
  • Local Syokimau shops (Greatwall area) – Some hardware and furniture stores offer competitive prices on curtains, rugs.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Do I need to spend on professional photography?

Yes – after furnishing, spend KSh 5,000‑8,000 on a photographer. It will boost your booking rate by 30‑50%, directly reducing payback time.

Should I buy or rent the apartment for Airbnb?

If you already own, great. If renting, get written permission from your landlord – many Syokimau leases prohibit short‑stays. A mortgage may be better long‑term.

What is the average cleaning cost per booking in Syokimau?

Between KSh 800 and 1,500, depending on size and whether you outsource. Many hosts include it in the nightly price to avoid sticker shock.
